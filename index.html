Edit360: 2D Image Edits to 3D Assets from Any Angle

Edit360 enables precise 360-degree 3D edits by applying tailored elements and styles to 3D models following instructions.

Abstract

The integration of diffusion models has advanced image generation and editing, but extending these capabilities to 3D assets remains challenging, particularly for fine-grained edits requiring consistency across multiple perspectives. Existing methods often rely on fixed views or global prompts, limiting flexibility and coherence. We present Edit360, a novel paradigm for precise and consistent 360-degree 3D editing. Edit360 takes a single-view image and user-provided editing instructions as input, employing a large language model (LLM) to determine the optimal editing perspectives, referred to as anchor views. These anchor views are generated using multi-view generation techniques and edited with image editing tools to meet user requirements. To propagate 2D edits from anchor views across the full 360-degree range, Edit360 integrates a video diffusion model enhanced with our proposed Spatial Frame Fusion (SFF) algorithm. By aligning and merging information from multiple views within the residuals and latents of U-Nets, SFF ensures spatial coherence and structural consistency across multi-view sequences. The resulting multi-view sequences are then used to reconstruct high-quality, edited 3D assets, enabling diverse 3D asset customizations in animation, gaming and virtual reality.

Our Method and Pipline

Edit360 enables precise and consistent 3D asset editing from arbitrary viewpoints by combining large language models (LLMs), image editing, and a novel spatial fusion strategy within video diffusion.

The pipeline starts with selecting and editing anchor views based on user instructions, guided by an LLM to identify optimal viewing angles. The edited anchor views and the front view are processed through our Spatial Frame Fusion (SFF) algorithm, which combines latent space fusion and deep-guided fusion to propagate edits consistently across all angles. The resulting multi-view sequence can be reconstructed into a fully edited 3D asset via mesh extraction or 3D Gaussian splatting.
